Information

  • Scientific Name Panthera leo
  • Place Origin Sub-Saharan Africa; Small Asiatic Population in India
  • History Once widespread across Eurasia; revered in many ancient cultures. Lions are the only truly social big cats, forming prides that work together for hunting and protection.
  • Size 150-250 kg; 1.1-1.2 m shoulder height
  • Life Span 10-14 years in the wild; up to 20 years in captivity
  • Characteristics Muscular build, golden coat, tufted tail, and for males, a distinctive mane that signals dominance and maturity.
  • Temperament Cooperative, territorial, and protective of their pride. Females handle most of the hunting.

Fun Fact!

Did you know? A lion's roar can be heard up to 8 km away, used to mark territory and communicate.
